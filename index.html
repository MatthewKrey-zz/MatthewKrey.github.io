Passionate. Mindful. Curious.
Learning to code. Hungry to build.

I graduated from the University of Pennsylvania in 2012 with an interdisciplinary degree in Neuroscience and Biology. While a varsity swimmer, pre-med student and undergraduate research technician, I became intensely curious about the future of healthcare. After four years with my head either underwater, behind a microscope, or hovering over textbooks thick enough to help a one-legged pirate keep their sea-legs, I learned that med school was only one of many routes to impacting healthcare.

From trauma calls as an EMT, to teaching elementary school special education, to working with pharmaceutical executives on global risk management, R&D, employee health and sustainability - I have since learned that there are no shortcuts to improving human health.

I recently decided to hang up my suit as a pharmaceutical management trainee at GlaxoSmithKline and pursue software development as a student at The Flatiron School.
